    4. What is Partition of Property?

    Partition means the division of property held by joint co-owners, into different shares, so that they can enjoy exclusive rights over their property.



    Kinds of Properties that can be partitioned

    1. Joint Family Property

    2. Self-Acquired Property

    (2) Self-Acquired Property:

    A property is a self-acquired property when it is earned or acquired by the personal effort of any person. In the case of self-acquired property, the person who earns or acquires the property is the single owner of the property, and no one other than the person himself can exercise his/her any right over the property during his/her lifetime.

    A property acquired by a Hindu on his/her own and by his/her own efforts is his/her separate property as it is not the result of any joint labor of other members of the Joint Hindu Family.

    Property that has been inherited by a Hindu from any person other than his father, grandfather, or great grandfather would have the same effect as a self-acquired property for the person inheriting the property. In the case where a person earns money through practicing a hereditary profession, the property acquired by that money will be his/her self-acquired and separate property and not joint property. A property acquired by a Hindu by a grant from the Government shall also be regarded as a separate property of that person.
